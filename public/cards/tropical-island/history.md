### The Dual Land Innovation (1993)

Richard Garfield designed dual lands as the ultimate mana fixing:
- Play any two-color deck smoothly
- No penalty for flexibility
- Basic land types for synergy

### Original Intent

Garfield assumed:
- Cards would be scarce
- Not everyone would own dual lands
- This justified their power level

Reality: Players collected playsets, making them staples.

### Reserved List Protection (1996)

All original dual lands were added to the Reserved List:
- Never to be reprinted
- Functional reprints banned
- Value protected forever

### "Fetchable" Power

When fetch lands were printed (Onslaught 2002, Zendikar 2009):
- Dual lands became even stronger
- Turn 1: Fetch â†’ Tropical Island, untapped
- Perfect mana without life loss

### Legacy Format Impact

Tropical Island is core to:
- **Infect**: Quick blue/green combo kills
- **RUG Delver**: Tempo threats + countermagic
- **Bant Miracles**: Control with green splash

### The Playability Premium

Unlike Power 9 (mostly collectibles), dual lands are:
- Actually played in tournaments
- Worn from shuffling
- Near Mint copies rarer than expected
