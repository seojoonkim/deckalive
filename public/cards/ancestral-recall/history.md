### 1993: The Beginning

Richard Garfield designed Ancestral Recall as one of the "boons"â€”a cycle of powerful 1-mana spells:

| Boon | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Ancestral Recall | Draw 3 cards |
| Lightning Bolt | Deal 3 damage |
| Dark Ritual | Add 3 black mana |
| Giant Growth | +3/+3 |
| Healing Salve | Gain 3 life or prevent 3 damage |

Garfield intentionally made Blue's boon the strongest because he underestimated card advantage.

### 1994: Restricted

Only one year after release, Wizards of the Coast restricted Ancestral Recall in Type I (now Vintage). It was clear the card was too powerful.

### 1996: Reserve List

When the Reserve List was created, Ancestral Recall was immediately included. This guaranteed it would never be reprinted, cementing its collectible value.

### 2000s: Legacy and Value

As competitive Legacy and Vintage scenes grew, demand for Power 9 increased. Ancestral Recall became both a tournament staple and investment piece.

### 2020s: Record Prices

The pandemic trading card boom pushed Alpha Power 9 to unprecedented prices. High-grade copies regularly sell for six figures.
